What Grok is great at — and where Super goes further

Grok

Grok is a fast‑moving assistant and agent platform from xAI. Grok 4.3 showed a major jump in real‑world agentic benchmarks, reaching an ELO of 1,500 on GDPval‑AA, a respected measure of task completion under realistic conditions (theaijournal.co).

  • Strong instruction following on customer‑support style benchmarks
  • Large context windows and configurable reasoning depth
  • Well suited to coding agents, research pipelines, and one‑off agentic tasks

Super

Super is focused on durable computer‑use workflows. Its defining advantage is a reusable computer-use cache, so when an agent repeats the same browser or desktop actions, it doesn’t start from scratch every time.

  • Agents that actually operate real computers
  • Cache reuse makes repeated workflows faster and cheaper over time
  • Designed for ongoing operational work, not just single runs

Why computer use — not just benchmarks — matters

Benchmarks show intent

Grok 4.3’s gains on GDPval‑AA highlight a clear shift toward agentic task completion, even if it trades off some non‑hallucination scores compared to earlier versions (theaijournal.co).

The market is moving to computer use

Google made computer use a first‑class capability inside Gemini 3.5 Flash, underlining how valuable real browser and desktop control has become for agents (blog.google).

Demand is real

Consumer interest in personal AI agents is growing quickly across Asia‑Pacific, suggesting this is more than a novelty chatbot trend (bwmarketingworld.com), (ndtvprofit.com).

Super’s bet

As agents move from talking to acting, the cost of repeating computer work matters. Super’s cache‑first design directly targets that problem for teams and individuals running the same workflows every day.
